  <h1>What is an SEO report?</h1><p class="">A basic SEO report is a summary of how your website is performing in search engines. It consolidates all your website data and analytics into one place, displaying how your customers and potential customers interact with your site.</p><p class="">An SEO report answers three key questions:</p><ul><li><p class="">How are customers finding your website?</p></li><li><p class="">What are they doing once they arrive there?</p></li><li><p class="">What can be tweaked to improve customer experience?</p></li></ul><p class="">An SEO report is a technical document, but think of it as a performance dashboard for your website.</p><h2>What’s included in an SEO report?</h2><p class="">SEO reports typically include a handful of core metrics. Understanding what these metrics are is the first step to utilizing them effectively. Here are some <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/seo-glossary" target="_blank"><span>useful definitions</span></a> and what they’re meant to show you.</p><p class=""><a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-drive-traffic-to-your-website" target="_blank"><span><strong>Organic Traffic</strong></span></a>: The number of visitors that arrive at your website via unpaid search results. This number tells you whether your website is visible in search results and whether that visibility is growing or shrinking.</p><p class=""><a href="https://support.squarespace.com/hc/en-us/articles/360001014647-Search-keywords-analytics" target="_blank"><span><strong>Keyword Rankings</strong></span></a>: Where your pages rank in search results for specific keywords or phrases. Keyword rankings show how visible your content is for target searches and which topics or keywords you’re gaining authority in.</p><p class=""><strong>Click-Through Rate (CTR)</strong>: How often users click over to your site after seeing search results. This shows you how enticing your titles and descriptions are—and whether they align with what people are actually searching for.</p><p class=""><strong>Bounce Rate</strong>: Bounce rate measures the percentage of visitors who leave your site without interacting further (navigating around the menu, clicking links, etc.). A higher bounce rate shows that there’s a mismatch between a searcher’s expectations and the actual content on your site.</p><p class=""><strong>Backlinks</strong>: Links from other sites that point to your content. The more sources that reference your site, the more discoverable it is across the web. Backlinks show your level of credibility and authority.</p><h1>SEO performance dashboards</h1><p class="">While a static report gives you a snapshot in time, a dashboard provides a living view of your website’s performance updated minute to minute, or at least regularly. Instead of combing through spreadsheets, you can quickly view traffic trends, best-performing pages, keyword changes, and conversions.</p><p class="">Dashboards make SEO actionable because they offer a way to quickly spot what is happening so you can address any issues early, identify opportunities, and make improvements.</p><p class="">If you’re on a <a href="https://support.squarespace.com/hc/en-us/articles/115005308187-What-s-my-site-s-version-and-template" target="_blank">7.1 Squarespace site,</a> you can find your dashboard by following these steps:</p><ol><li><p class="">Go to your Squarespace sidebar menu</p></li><li><p class="">Navigate to Website → SEO/AIO</p></li></ol><p class="">You can click on the top right Optimize button for <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/ai-seo" target="_blank"><span>AI-generated SEO improvements</span></a> to your site like meta data and <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-write-alt-text"><span>alt text</span></a>.</p><p class="">If you have a <a href="https://support.squarespace.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038270572-Moving-from-Squarespace-version-7-0-to-version-7-1" target="_blank"><span>7.0 site</span></a>, you’ll find the dashboard here:</p><ol><li><p class="">Go to your Squarespace sidebar menu</p></li><li><p class="">Navigate to Analytics → Traffic</p></li><li><p class="">Adjust the drop-down menu in the right corner to change the visits graph below&nbsp;</p></li></ol><p class="">The tab to the right of Traffic shows the traffic sources—direct, search, referral, social, etc. The next tab to the right of that is Search Keywords, and can be connected to Google Search Console.&nbsp;</p><p class="">You can use this dashboard data the same way you would a static SEO report.&nbsp;</p><h1>How to read an SEO report</h1><p class="">This is where people get stuck. The challenge isn’t just understanding what the core metrics are; it’s putting them in context and connecting them to real-world decisions. An SEO report is a story about how your website is performing over time. Learn to read that story and turn the data into actionable insights.</p><h2>Focus on trends over time</h2><p class="">It’s not very useful to focus on single data points. What happened yesterday or this week rarely tells you anything helpful on its own. SEO performance varies for many reasons—<a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/seo-trends" target="_blank"><span>algorithm changes</span></a>, customer behavior, seasonality, and many other squishy factors. What matters is how your statistics are trending over time.</p><p class="">Compare performance over longer periods, month over month or year over year, or before and after a specific change you’ve made. A 15% drop in traffic over a few days might not be cause for concern, but if traffic is dipping steadily over a matter of months, that requires your attention.</p><h2>Check what’s improving, declining, or leveling off</h2><p class="">Once you can spot the trends over time, it’s time to <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/getting-started-with-advanced-seo" target="_blank"><span>analyze what you’re seeing</span></a>.</p><p class="">Pages that are improving by gaining traction, keyword rankings, or engagement show positive momentum. It’s an opportunity to double down by creating similar content or building links between related pages on your site. These are things that strengthen your SEO.</p><p class="">Alternatively, declining performance signals that something needs your attention. If your page rankings are slipping or engagement is dropping, it might be time to <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/seo-mistakes" target="_blank"><span>refresh your content</span></a>, try different keywords, or improve your site’s structure to make it easier for search crawlers to read.</p><p class="">Content that’s holding steady can go either way. It says you’re remaining consistent, but also there’s a chance to be more competitive or more relevant.</p>

  <h2>​Connect metrics to customer behavior</h2><p class="">Metrics are important, but they’re only a reflection of how real humans interact with your website. Insights come when you connect those data points to customer behavior.</p><p class=""><strong>High ranking/low CTR: </strong>If you have a strong page rank on search engines but your click through is relatively weak, it implies that people are seeing your listing but choosing not to click. This might indicate that your <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-write-meta-descriptions-and-titles" target="_blank"><span>title or meta description</span></a> isn’t enticing enough, or doesn’t meet your customer’s needs. There’s a disconnect between what people are searching for and what you’re supplying.</p><p class=""><strong>High traffic/high bounce rate: </strong>If your bounce rate is high but traffic on the page remains strong, it could mean that your content isn’t meeting expectations or your positioning is unclear. Start by revisiting the title and meta description to make sure it accurately reflects what’s on the page.</p><p class=""><strong>Low rankings/low impressions</strong>: If your page isn’t getting many impressions and also ranks poorly in search results, it shows you that search engines don’t see your page as a sturdy or relevant result for that topic. This is not a performance issue so much as a visibility problem. Focus on firming up the page’s foundational SEO qualities. Refine your keyword targeting and flesh out your content to better serve the topic so that it correlates to <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/search-intent" target="_blank"><span>search intent</span></a>. <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/on-page-seo" target="_blank"><span>Build internal links</span></a>, make sure <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/visual-hierarchy" target="_blank"><span>your headings and site structure</span></a> make sense, and focus on <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/seo-tips-to-grow-your-site-traffic" target="_blank"><span>earning those backlinks</span></a>. All of these moves help increase authority so your page is seen.</p><p class="">Once you start connecting the dots and putting your SEO report in the context of real customers searching for something, it can tell you a lot about where to put your energy.</p><h1>Leveraging SEO insights into growth</h1><p class="">SEO data isn’t just to troubleshoot underperforming pages. It can also guide your business decision-making to areas of growth. This is where these reports really shine.</p><h2>Identifying high-potential keywords</h2><p class="">Look in your SEO report for keywords that rank on page two or the bottom of page one in search engines. These are your best bets for wins. Expand the content around those keywords, connect pages on your site with internal links, and add depth or updated information. Any move up the page rankings can increase traffic exponentially.</p><h2>Use SEO data to guide content strategy</h2><p class="">Your SEO report or dashboard shows exactly what people are searching for, so create content around high-performing topics. <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-do-keyword-research" target="_blank"><span>Use keywords across various pieces</span></a> of content and pages, and be sure to build content that strengthens your best-performing pages.</p><h2>Use Engagement Data to Improve Content</h2><p class="">Engagement data shows what action your customers take after they arrive on your website—whether they leave right away, scroll, or interact by clicking links. If your engagement is low, your content may not be showing the answers a searcher is looking for. If key points are buried in the text, readers may lose interest. Keep your text focused on delivering information promised by your keywords.</p><h1>How Squarespace supports SEO tracking</h1><p class="">For business owners managing their own websites, one challenge is consolidating data from multiple tools. <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/squarespace-ai-seo-tools" target="_blank"><span>Squarespace makes this simple</span></a> by integrating key performance indicators directly into your <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/improving-seo-on-your-squarespace-website" target="_blank"><span>website platform</span></a>. Once you understand how to read them, patterns start to emerge, so you can take consistent action. The goal isn’t to track everything; it’s to focus more on what matters and make meaningful improvements over time.</p><p class="">If you get in the habit of reviewing your SEO reports regularly and use them to guide your decisions around content, SEO stops being a jumble of numbers and starts becoming a reliable driver of growth for your website.</p>
