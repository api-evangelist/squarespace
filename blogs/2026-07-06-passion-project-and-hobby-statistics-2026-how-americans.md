Increasingly, creative work in the United States sits at the intersection of leisure, side income, and entrepreneurship. Whether it’s photography, digital design, crafting, or launching a small online shop, Americans are dedicating measurable time each week to activities that often evolve into passion projects. Public data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics’ American Time Use Survey (ATUS) helps quantify this shift, showing how people actually spend their time across work, leisure, and creative activities.
