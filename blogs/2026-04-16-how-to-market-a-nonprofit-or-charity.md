<p class=""><a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-start-a-nonprofit-or-charity" target="_blank">Running a nonprofit or charity </a>means you're doing work that matters—but it only reaches its full potential when the right people know about it. Whether you're trying to grow your donor base, recruit volunteers, build community trust, or raise awareness for your cause, marketing is one of the most powerful tools you have. The good news is that you don't need a big budget or a large team to do it well.</p><p class="">This guide walks you through the key strategies for marketing a nonprofit or charity: from building a clear, mission-driven brand to cultivating the donors, volunteers, and community partners who will help your organization grow. Every section focuses on practical steps you can take with the resources you have right now.</p>

    <span>Key takeaways
  </h3>

  <ul class="sq-key-takeaways__list">

    <li><strong>Search visibility helps people find your cause when they're looking to give, volunteer, or get involved.</strong></li>
    <li><strong>Educational content and storytelling are especially powerful for nonprofits, where the "why" drives action.</strong></li>
    <li><strong>Social proof—donor testimonials, volunteer stories, and impact data—builds credibility and inspires others to join.</strong></li>

  <h2>Establish an authentic brand identity</h2><p class="">For a nonprofit or charity, <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/brand-visual-identity" target="_blank"><span>brand identity isn't about looking polished</span></a>. It's about communicating your mission with clarity and conviction. Donors, volunteers, and community partners need to understand who you are, what you do, and why it matters the moment they land on your website or come across your name.</p><p class="">Start by getting specific about what sets your organization apart. There are many nonprofits doing meaningful work in every space. Think about your approach, your community, your particular focus. That distinction belongs in <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/branding-strategies-for-small-businesses" target="_blank"><span>your messaging, your visuals</span></a>, and every piece of content you put into the world.</p><p class="">Your tone matters, too. A children's literacy nonprofit will communicate differently from a disaster relief fund, even if both are doing urgent, important work. Once you've identified the voice that fits your organization, apply it consistently across <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/launching-an-effective-nonprofit-site" target="_blank">your website</a>, your <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/squarespace-donation-tools" target="_blank"><span>donation pages</span></a>, your social profiles, and your newsletters. If you're not sure where to start, <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/ai-website-copy-generator" target="_blank">Squarespace's AI writing tools</a> can help you draft site copy, mission statements, and service descriptions that you then shape in your own voice.</p>

  <h2>Boost search visibility</h2><p class="">When someone wants to support a cause, donate to a local charity, or find volunteer opportunities, they often start with a search. If your organization doesn't show up, that potential supporter finds another charity. Strong search visibility means your cause is findable when it matters most.</p><p class="">Squarespace's <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/squarespace-ai-seo-tools" target="_blank"><span>built-in SEO and AEO tools</span></a> help you optimize your website for both traditional search engines and the AI-powered platforms that people increasingly use to discover organizations like yours. AEO, <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/ai-search-seo" target="_blank"><span>answer engine optimization</span></a>, is about making sure your content answers the specific questions your audience is asking, so your site surfaces in AI-generated summaries and responses, beyond standard search results.</p><h3>Research and integrate keywords</h3><p class=""><a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-optimize-your-website-for-search-engines" target="_blank"><span>Keywords for a nonprofit</span></a> might include phrases like "food bank in [city]," "how to donate to animal rescue," or "volunteer opportunities near me." Think about <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/search-intent" target="_blank"><span>how your donors and supporters describe what you do</span></a> when they're searching for it, not how you describe it internally.</p><p class="">Weave those phrases naturally into your page headings, donation descriptions, program summaries, and <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/seo-glossary" target="_blank"><span>metadata</span></a>. The goal is to sound helpful and human, not keyword-stuffed. A good rule of thumb: Write for the person, and let Squarespace's SEO tools handle the technical optimization.</p><h2>Create educational or empowering content</h2><p class="">Nonprofits and charities have a natural advantage when it comes to <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/create-quality-content" target="_blank"><span>content marketing</span></a>: you have stories worth telling. Publishing content that educates your audience about your cause, shares impact data, or explains how donations are used can turn casual visitors into committed supporters.</p><p class="">Content also keeps your <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/audience-development" target="_blank"><span>existing community engaged</span></a> between fundraising campaigns. A donor who reads your blog regularly is far more likely to give again—and to share your work with others.</p><h3>The power of the nonprofit blog</h3><p class="">A blog gives your organization a home for the stories and information that make people care. You can use it to share program updates, highlight the communities you serve, explain the systemic issues your organization addresses, and show donors exactly what their contributions make possible.</p><p class="">A regularly updated blog also improves your search visibility over time, giving people more pathways to find you. If keeping up with posts feels like a stretch, <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/ai-blogging" target="_blank">Squarespace's AI writing tools</a> can help you get a first draft on the page faster, so the blank-page problem doesn't slow you down. You still bring the voice, the mission, and the nuance—the tool just helps you get started.</p><h3>Newsletters as a marketing tool</h3><p class=""><a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-create-a-newsletter" target="_blank"><span>Email newsletters</span></a> are one of the most reliable ways to stay connected with your supporters. For a nonprofit, a well-timed newsletter can deepen donor relationships, announce campaigns, share impact updates, and bring lapsed supporters back into the fold.</p><p class="">Aim for consistency over frequency—monthly is a good starting point for most organizations. When drafting, focus on one story or theme per issue rather than trying to cover everything at once. <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/write-emails-with-ai" target="_blank">Squarespace's AI writing tools</a> can also help you draft and refine newsletter content, so you can get those emails out the door.</p><h2>Utilize social proof</h2><p class="">People give to organizations they trust. And nothing builds trust faster than hearing from others who have already given, volunteered, or been helped. <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/guide-to-social-proof" target="_blank"><span>Social proof—testimonials, reviews, impact numbers, and community stories</span></a>—is one of the most persuasive tools in your marketing toolkit.</p><p class="">For nonprofits and charities, this can take many forms: a donor explaining why they give every year, a volunteer describing what a day of service looks like, a community member sharing what your programs have meant to them. All of it helps potential supporters answer the question of whether your organization is worth their time or money.</p>

  <h2>Gather testimonials from the people you serve</h2><p class=""><a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/client-testimonials-marketing-strategy" target="_blank"><span>The most powerful testimonials a nonprofit can have</span></a> come from the community members you’re supporting. A parent who found stable housing through your programs, a student who received tutoring and went on to graduate, a family that had enough to eat because of your food pantry—those stories carry a weight that no statistic can match.</p><p class="">Collect these testimonials in natural, unhurried moments: when someone shares good news with your team, when a program milestone is reached, or during a follow-up conversation after a service is completed. Keep the ask low-pressure. Just a few sentences about what the experience meant to them is more than enough.</p><p class="">Display these stories prominently on your website, especially on your donation and "get involved" pages, where they can show potential supporters exactly who their contributions reach. Share them in newsletters, on social media, and in grant applications. All of it helps potential supporters answer the question: “Should I give this organization my time or money?"</p><h2>Personalize marketing efforts</h2><p class="">Not every person in your network has the same relationship with your organization. A first-time visitor who just learned about your cause needs different communication than a donor who's given for five years. A volunteer who's on-site every weekend has different interests than someone who's only engaged online. Tailoring your outreach to match those differences makes your marketing feel relevant rather than generic.</p><p class=""><a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/what-is-customer-segmentation" target="_blank"><span>Segment your audience</span></a> into groups that make sense for your organization: major donors, recurring donors, one-time donors, volunteers, newsletter subscribers, and event attendees are a few common categories. Then shape your messaging so each group feels seen.</p><h3>Automate emails to engage and re-engage supporters</h3><p class=""><a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/email-automation" target="_blank"><span>Automated emails let you stay in touch</span></a> at the moments that matter most: a welcome message when someone joins your <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/get-email-subscribers" target="_blank"><span>mailing list</span></a>, a thank-you after a donation, a follow-up after a volunteer shift, or a re-engagement email for supporters you haven't heard from in a while. You can personalize these messages so they feel relevant rather than generic.</p><p class="">For a nonprofit, automation is especially valuable because it keeps communication consistent without requiring manual effort from a small team. A donor who gets a warm, timely thank you right after giving feels appreciated—and is more likely to give again.</p><h2>Develop thought leadership</h2><p class="">One of the most effective long-term marketing strategies for a nonprofit is positioning your leadership team and staff as credible voices in your space. When your executive director speaks at a panel, writes an op-ed, or appears on a podcast about your cause, it gives a strong boost to your organization's reputation.</p><p class="">Look for opportunities to contribute to the conversations your donors, partners, and community members are already having. Submit essays to local publications. Speak at community events and conferences. Offer expert commentary to journalists covering your issue area. Share research or policy insights on your blog. Over time, these contributions build a body of work that signals expertise and draws people to your organization organically.</p><h2>Collect leads</h2><p class="">Your website might attract thousands of visitors during a campaign or giving season, but if those visitors leave without any connection to your organization, that traffic doesn't convert into lasting support. Your goal is to turn visitors into contacts you can continue to reach directly.</p><p class="">A contact form on your website does double duty: It gives visitors an easy way to reach you and adds their information to your list so you can follow up. Consider placing forms at key moments, such as after someone reads your impact report, finishes a blog post, or learns about a specific program.</p><p class="">You can also offer something valuable in exchange for <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/how-to-start-your-email-list" target="_blank"><span>contact information</span></a>, like a resource kit related to your cause or an invitation to a virtual Q&amp;A with your leadership team. These offers give potential supporters a reason to raise their hand—and give you a contact to follow up with.</p><h2>Lean into community-based marketing</h2><p class="">Nonprofits and charities are, by nature, rooted in community. That's a marketing advantage. Participating in local events, co-hosting programs with other organizations, and showing up in the spaces where your audience already gathers can build your reputation in ways that <a href="https://www.squarespace.com/blog/organic-marketing" target="_blank"><span>digital outreach</span></a> alone can't.</p><p class="">Sponsor a local 5K. Book a table at a farmers market or community fair. Partner with a library or school to host an educational event. Join local Facebook groups or neighborhood platforms where people are already talking about issues related to your cause. Show up consistently, contribute with real purpose, and let your presence do the work. For organizations with a national or global focus, the same principles apply in online communities—forums, social groups, and issue-specific networks where your audience is already engaged.</p><h2>Create strategic partnerships</h2><p class="">Partnering with complementary organizations—local businesses, schools, faith communities, or allied nonprofits—can extend your reach, add credibility, and bring new audiences to your cause. A restaurant that donates a portion of Tuesday night proceeds to your food pantry reaches their entire customer base on your behalf. A local boutique that joins forces with you to host a coat drive expands your efforts and raises awareness of your cause.</p><p class="">When identifying potential partners, look for alignment on values and audience rather than just visibility. The most productive partnerships are the ones where both sides mutually benefit and share a community. A co-hosted event, a joint campaign, or a simple cross-promotion in each other's newsletters can open doors to a whole new audience.</p><h2>Bring it all together</h2><p class="">Marketing your nonprofit or charity successfully means showing people why your work matters—and making it easy for them to be part of it. The strategies in this guide work together: a clear brand identity makes your content more compelling, strong SEO brings new supporters to that content, and the right tools help you convert that interest into lasting relationships. Start where you have capacity, build consistency over time, and trust that every connection you make today is part of the community your organization will rely on tomorrow.</p>
